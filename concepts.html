Core Web3 Concepts

Understand the fundamentals through side-by-side comparisons

Web2

  • Centralized control
  • Company-owned servers
  • Users are the product
  • Data in private databases
  • Permission required
VS

Web3

  • Decentralized network
  • Blockchain-based
  • User-owned data
  • Transparent & verifiable
  • Permissionless access

Bitcoin

  • Digital currency
  • Store of value ("digital gold")
  • Simple transactions
  • Proof of Work (PoW)
  • Limited scripting
VS

Ethereum

  • Programmable blockchain
  • Smart contracts
  • DApps & DeFi platform
  • Proof of Stake (PoS)
  • Turing-complete

Public Key

  • Like your bank account number
  • Safe to share publicly
  • Used to receive funds
  • Derives your wallet address
  • Visible on blockchain
VS

Private Key

  • Like your password/PIN
  • NEVER share it
  • Used to sign transactions
  • If lost = funds lost forever
  • Controls your assets

Traditional Database

  • Centralized control
  • Editable by admin
  • Requires trust in operator
  • Single point of failure
  • Closed & proprietary
VS

Blockchain

  • Decentralized network
  • Immutable (write-once)
  • Trustless verification
  • No single point of failure
  • Transparent & open
